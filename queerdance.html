Queer Dance

Explore how you can mix up your dance to best express yourself

How do you “queer” dance?

“To queer” something means to challenge the heteronormativity of it in society. What does that mean for dance? One way I think about it is related to the roles the dancers take.

In partnered dance in this society, men tend to take a “leader” role, guiding and directing the movements, and women tend to “follow,” responding to and adding their own interpretation and flare to carry out the movements. This could be described as the leader drawing the picture and the follower coloring (mostly) within the lines.

When we queer partner dance, we can play with leader and follower roles with respect to gender, and if and to what extent we use them. For example, the leader could be any gender (woman, non-binary, man, etc). The follower could be any gender as well. The dancers could switch roles at specific moments in the dance or constantly throughout the dance. We could have more than two people dancing together, simultaneously or at different times in the dance. There are no rules, and sky’s the limit. Let’s step outside the box and get creative!

Unless you like the box. I can also teach within the box.

Some people want a routine they can do with ease and comfort, and others are looking for something that challenges them. Some people are looking for both. I work with your interests to provide moves, techniques, and routines that meet your dance goals.
